Event 1

Reflection “FCAJ Community Day - Conference Call”

Event Purpose

  • Share best practices on how to build and apply AI in real-world work
  • Introduce new AWS services and products related to AI, data, and cloud infrastructure
  • Inspire through case studies, hackathons, and stories of building real products
  • Connect students, developers, and experts from the industry

Speaker List

  • Huynh Hoang Long - Event host
  • Tran Dai Vi - Event host
  • Together with guest speakers from AWS and the technology community:
  • Vy Lam - Senior Business Systems Analyst, VPBank
  • Thao Nguyen - GenAI Engineer, VIB
  • Mai Nguyen - GenAI Engineer, VIB
  • Uyen Le - GenAI Engineer, VIB
  • Anh Pham - Cloud Consultant, G-AsiaPacific Vietnam
  • Thinh Nguyen - DevOps Engineer, FCAJ
  • Tinh Truong - Platform Engineer, GoTymeX
  • Duc Dao - Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics

Key Highlights

Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You

  • Why AI fails without context, and what “context” really means
  • From prompt to memory: how AI is evolving (the concept of the Second AI Brain)
  • How better context leads to better results (thinking and practical tips)
  • Career guidance and how students can start with AI, followed by Q&A

36 Hours with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality

  • Why I joined LotusHacks
  • The brainstorming journey from zero to an idea
  • Identifying the problem and shaping the UTMorpho product
  • The pressure of building a product in 36 hours
  • Challenges, failures, and turning points
  • An overview demo of the UTMorpho product
  • Lessons learned and next directions

From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation

  • Amazon CloudFront for all types of workloads
  • Cost optimization with Amazon CloudFront
  • Security features
  • Improving reliability with Amazon CloudFront
  • Improving performance with Amazon CloudFront

Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick

  • Quick Chat Agent: an AI assistant to explore data and analyze insights
  • Quick Flows: create intelligent workflows using natural language without coding
  • Quick Spaces: a collaboration space that turns personal insights into team knowledge
  • Quick Sight: build dashboards and reports from raw data using natural language

Non-Determinism of “Deterministic” LLM Settings

  • How the LLM chooses the next token
  • Assumption: Temperature = 0 guarantees determinism
  • Reality: inference optimizations can still produce different results
  • Real-world impacts
  • Mitigation strategies

Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring

  • The gap between traditional banking systems and startup data
  • Single Agent: when to use it and when not to
  • Multi-Agent paradigm
  • Blueprint of a “virtual credit committee”
  • Guardrails and compliance
  • Operational ROI and rollout plan

What I Learned

AI Thinking and Context

  • Context is the decisive factor: AI works effectively only when provided with sufficient and appropriate context
  • Second AI Brain: understanding how AI is moving from single prompts to continuous memory and context
  • Building products under pressure: lessons from the 36-hour hackathon on brainstorming, making quick decisions, and handling failure

Architecture and Infrastructure

  • CloudFront for every workload: a better understanding of how to optimize cost, security, performance, and reliability when using a CDN
  • Multi-Agent System: understanding when a single agent is enough and when a more complex multi-agent architecture is needed
  • Guardrails and Compliance: the importance of controlling risk when deploying AI in sensitive domains such as credit scoring

Operating AI in Practice

  • Amazon Quick: the Chat Agent, Flows, Spaces, and Sight components help businesses use data more effectively
  • Non-determinism of LLMs: recognizing that temperature = 0 does not guarantee identical results, and that appropriate mitigation strategies are needed

Application to Work

  • Improve AI context: build a better process for providing context when working with AI tools in projects
  • Apply CloudFront: consider using a CDN to optimize cost and performance for applications being deployed
  • Experiment with Amazon Quick: pilot Quick Chat/Flows/Sight features for internal data analysis
  • Design multi-agent systems: use the virtual credit committee blueprint as a reference for problems that require multiple coordinated agents
  • Be mindful of non-determinism: build appropriate tests and validation processes when using LLMs for tasks that require high stability

Experience in the Event

Participating in “FCAJ Community Day - Conference Call” was an exciting experience that gave me access to new perspectives on AI, cloud infrastructure, and how to build real products. Some of the most memorable experiences were:

Learning from speakers and real stories

  • Listening directly to the organizing team and guest speakers share their views on AI, context engineering, and system architecture.
  • The 36-hour journey of building UTMorpho at LotusHacks helped me understand the process from idea to product under time pressure.

Practical technical experience

  • Gaining a deeper understanding of Amazon CloudFront and how to optimize cost, security, and performance for systems.
  • Exploring Amazon Quick and its Chat Agent, Flows, Spaces, and Sight features for enterprise data exploration.
  • Understanding the non-deterministic nature of LLMs and the mitigation strategies used in practice.

Applying modern tools and architectures

  • Gaining exposure to multi-agent system architecture through the startup credit scoring case study, helping me imagine how to apply it to more complex problems.
  • Recognizing the importance of guardrails and compliance when deploying AI in sensitive fields.

Connection and exchange

  • The event created opportunities to connect with students, developers, and industry experts.
  • Through the Q&A sessions, I gained more insight into career direction and how to begin with AI.

Lessons learned

  • Context is the core factor that determines the quality of results when working with AI.
  • Choosing the right architecture (single-agent vs. multi-agent, CDN, serverless, etc.) needs to be based on the specific characteristics of the problem.
  • A strategy to control stability and risk is needed when bringing AI into important systems.
